WHO WE ARE

SheBloom is a regional empowerment initiative dedicated to ending period poverty in the Busoga region. We provide menstrual products deliver menstrual health education, empowering girls and women to lead healthier, more confident lives. In Busoga, every month, countless girls endure fear, shame, and isolation simply because of their periods. Some grow up being told that menstruation is a curse or a sign of misfortune, leaving them scared, confused, and ashamed of their own bodies. Others are ridiculed by classmates, called cruel names, or forced to stay home from school, missing out on the education they deserve. Many have never even seen a sanitary pad and are left to rely on old cloths, rags, or paper, risking their health, dignity, and dreams. These silent struggles steal opportunities, hope, and the chance to reach their full potential.

Witnessing these injustices firsthand, Mengi Mariam felt a calling she could not ignore. In 2024, she founded SheBloom, driven by the belief that no girl should feel ashamed of her body, miss school because of her period, or be treated as less than the remarkable person she is.

At SheBloom we believe every girl deserves better.

Through school workshops, community outreach, and pad distribution, we are:

  • Breaking the silence around menstruation.
  • Providing access to safe hygiene materials
  • Restoring dignity to girls who have been mocked or shamed

-- Because when a girl understands her body,she gains confidence.

-- When she can attend school every day, she keeps her dreams alive.

And when she blooms, her whole community blooms too

Our Mission

To ensure every girl across the Busoga region can face menstruation with dignity, stay in school, and reach her full potential through mestrual education, access to sanitary pads and community awereness.

Our Vision

A Busoga region where no girl is held back by her period where she is free to learn, dream, and thrive with dignity.
